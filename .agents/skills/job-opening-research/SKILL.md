# Job Opening Research

Find the openings a candidate should actually see — not just the ones easy keyword queries surface. The workflow is **discovery as a system**: thesis → universe → sources → log → self-audit, with every phase leaving an auditable artifact. `references/search-workflow.md` is normative — read it before running; it defines the artifact schemas, the ATS source matrix with URL patterns, the fallback ladder, and stopping criteria. Also read `references/search-source-registry.yaml` before building the company universe/source plan; use relevant curated company sets and ATS discovery patterns as seeds, verifying every URL at runtime. `references/recruiting-sheet-schema.md` governs store writes.

Work in the recruiting repo root; resolve the active search project via `active-project.json` (`references/project-structure.md`) — artifacts live under `<project>/targets/`.

## Why this structure exists

A previous live run missed directly relevant openings because discovery relied on the user's seed companies and title-keyword queries, and left no trace of what was considered. Result: misses were undiagnosable (unseen? seen-and-skipped?) and unrepeatable. The five artifacts fix both: they force breadth *before* searching and auditability *after*.

## Your judgment vs scripts

You (the agent) own every decision: interpreting the profile into a thesis, choosing which adjacent companies belong in the universe and why, picking sources, judging each posting, deciding when coverage is sufficient. Deterministic scripts own repeatable mechanics: `scripts/resolve_company_sources.py` plans official-first source order, `scripts/build_location_search_urls.py` expands registry location-format hints into search URL candidates, `scripts/fetch_url.py` fetches public pages/cache summaries, `scripts/normalize_job_url.py` canonicalizes URLs/job_ids, `scripts/location_eligibility.py` prevents exact-city over-filtering when a nearby metro role should be reviewed, `scripts/analyze_search_plan.py` checks plan readiness before capture, `scripts/merge_research_parts.py` merges shard logs, `scripts/generate_coverage_audit.py` writes deterministic coverage audit scaffolding, `scripts/analyze_search_coverage.py` classifies complete/partial/blocked capture, `scripts/probe_linkedin_jobs.py` records the runner-owned LinkedIn Jobs observation, `scripts/persist_job_rows.py` normalizes/validates/upserts rows, `scripts/validate_research_artifacts.py` checks search artifacts afterward, `scripts/validate_linkedin_review.py` checks LinkedIn prep artifacts, and `scripts/validate_interview_prep.py` checks interview prep artifacts. Do not write one-off Python for these common tasks; if a needed adapter is missing, report the capability gap rather than generating throwaway scripts. Do not read external helper skills; use this RoleScout skill, the referenced RoleScout files, and the deterministic scripts. The validators will never tell you which company to search; that reasoning is yours and must be written down as rationale.

## The five phases (artifacts mandatory)

1. **Opportunity thesis** → `targets/opportunity-thesis.md`. Role *families* (strategy/ops, BD/partnerships, GTM, product strategy, chief of staff, ecosystem, AI deployment...), seniority band, hard constraints, domain buckets, evidence-backed strengths/gaps. Confirm direction with the user if the project is new; otherwise derive from profile + project.json + scoring config. If `candidate-profile.md`/`evidence-map.md` do not exist yet, search still proceeds from declared project targets, LinkedIn/source hints, and user instructions; label profile-dependent fit assumptions as provisional and recommend `prep` after search to build the evidence-backed profile.
2. **Company universe** → `targets/company-universe.json`. Seeds are a floor, not a ceiling. **Treat every user-named company as an *example of a market map*, not only a literal target**: for each seed, infer its *archetype* (scale/maturity, business model, product category, talent pool, location market), then expand that archetype into close peers — direct competitors, same-talent-pool employers, tightly adjacent product categories, ecosystem partners, funded entrants, and location-relevant employers. Fix the relationship *types*, never specific company names. Name-brand seeds (large platforms, hyperscalers, frontier labs, category leaders) are the ones most often left un-expanded, but default expansion must remain conservative: a smaller high-confidence universe is better than a broad, loosely related market map, and there is no fixed company-count target. Use relevant `search-source-registry.yaml` company sets as seed/adjacency hints when they match the thesis, never as a mandatory universal list. Every company carries a rationale tied to the thesis, every exclusion carries a reason. **Default close-peer expansion**: add roughly 1-3 strong adjacent employers per seed when the relationship is clear; fewer is acceptable when the thesis is narrow and the bucket records why. More is acceptable only when the user asks for a broad sweep or the project/universe explicitly sets `expansion_mode: "broad"`. Candidate profile/history is not a domain-expansion engine: use it mainly for fit scoring and role interpretation, and add at most 1-2 profile-derived employers only when they are exceptionally close to the user-set seed market. Before saving, run one **omissions self-critique** — "given these seeds, role families, and locations, what obvious close peer employers are missing?" — and add each with rationale or exclude it with a reason (carry the result into `coverage-audit.md`). Weak adjacency is excluded by default unless explicitly requested: for an AI/platform/SEA superapp seed set, consumer community apps, music platforms, general e-commerce retailers, travel apps, and gaming publishers need a very specific rationale. FAANG-like platforms and close social-platform peers may still qualify when tied directly to the seed thesis.
3. **Source plan** -> `targets/source-plan.json`. Per company: trusted source path in trust order (official/registered careers source first when known -> ATS page only after official discovery or verified ATS token -> web discovery -> LinkedIn Jobs mandatory pass under the login-handoff contract -> boards as signal). Use `python scripts/resolve_company_sources.py "<Company>" --json` as the deterministic official-first starting point, then adapt it with your judgment. Registered companies must not start with guessed ATS slugs; verify their registry careers/listing/detail URLs first. **Self-hosted careers sites** - many employers publish NO third-party ATS board; that absence is *expected*, never a `failed_capture` on its own, and is *detected* (official careers/source resolver first, ATS-slug probes only as fallback), not assumed from a company's name. When there is no ATS board, go to the official careers site: consult the registry's `self_hosted_careers` and `major_company_careers` sections - curated examples (each with careers search endpoint, optional `location_search_url_template`, `posting_url` pattern, source kind, and `render` type; verify at runtime) - and use that adapter; for a company **not** in the registry, web-discover its careers site and apply the same method (registry location template when present -> careers JSON API / search endpoint -> enumerate -> capture each posting's own `posting_url` -> parse its JD). For registry location templates, use `python scripts/build_location_search_urls.py --company "<Company>" --location "<target>" --json`; do not hand-format one URL and stop. A zero-result or ignored-location page is a format-suspicion signal, so try primary multi-location, canonical single-location, and raw variants before declaring absence. Client-rendered (`render: js`) sites need supported browser tooling; if browser tooling is unavailable in the current environment, record the connector/browser gap and continue the rest of the source plan. Server-rendered / JSON-API sites need no browser. Try ATS URL patterns from the reference and registry directly only as fallback/discovery signals. Use `python scripts/fetch_url.py <url> --out <project>/data/<source>.json --json` for JSON feeds; stdout is a compact summary, not the raw feed. Prefer the branded careers URL as `source_url` when one exists (registry `branded_url_rule`), and check the registry's `posting_expiry_signals` before persisting. Aggregator boards/feeds in the registry's `job_boards_and_aggregators` are discovery signals - verify and capture at the company/ATS source. On structured boards, apply source-supported target-location filters first, enumerate the returned listing set, judge each returned posting, and use bulk roll-up entries for out-of-location/out-of-family skips. Do not fetch every worldwide JD body unless the source lacks usable filters and listing metadata is insufficient. Never keyword-filter a board at the source. **Fallback ladder is enforced**: `failed_capture` is legal only after >=3 distinct source types, recorded in order. A JS-blocked page, empty static HTML, DNS/network block, missing browser runtime, authwall, verification prompt, or connector error is a fallback trigger, never absence evidence; use `pending_fallback` (reason `run_interrupted`) until another source family or browser-rendered pass resolves it.
4. **Candidate log** → `targets/research-log.json`. Every candidate seen anywhere — `kept/skipped/failed_capture` + reason code; queries with `results_seen` (≤1 results = logged coverage signal, not evidence of absence). Capture per posting: company, exact title, location, remote policy, seniority, must-have vs nice-to-have, 2–3 sentence JD summary, **direct posting URL**, date. Listing/search URLs (for example Google Careers `.../jobs/results?q=...` or ServiceNow `/jobs/`) belong only in `queries`, never as a persisted `kept` job URL. To keep a candidate, resolve the listing/card/API result to the posting's own URL, fetch or render that posting, and snapshot its JD to `targets/jobs/<job_id>.json`; the snapshot must include full JD text in `raw_text`, `jd_text`, `job_description`, `description`, `content`, `body_text`, or `html`, not only an LLM summary. If the title is interesting or user-forced but the direct posting URL/JD cannot be verified, record `pending_fallback` (reason `run_interrupted`) instead of `kept`; do not persist placeholder "Not verified" rows. Never enter credentials or bypass access controls yourself. The LinkedIn Jobs pass runs LAST, after phases 1-3 and all non-login sources; declare it blocked only after navigating to linkedin.com/jobs and recording the observed state in the log (`jobs_page_ok` = proceed). If blockage is observed, finish and persist everything else first, then STOP with the `APPROVAL_REQUIRED` handoff from `references/search-workflow.md` — the user signs in, and the rerun executes only the LinkedIn increment.
5. **Coverage self-audit** → `targets/coverage-audit.md`. Thin buckets and why, dead-ended fallbacks, low-coverage signals, prioritized next searches. Run `python scripts/analyze_search_plan.py <project>` before capture if you are running manually, then `python scripts/analyze_search_coverage.py <project>` after capture/finalize. Your user-facing summary quotes this — never report done over unexplained thin buckets or a PARTIAL/BLOCKED coverage gate.

Stopping criteria are coverage goals (every company attempted; every family×location queried; marginal yield <2 new candidates over last 3 queries), not query quotas — budgets in the reference are defaults the thesis may override, and running out of budget is reported, not silently absorbed.

## Parallel capture (universes >~15 companies)

Shard Phase 4 by company with a bounded parallel worker pool per the protocol in `references/search-workflow.md` ("Parallel capture with subagents"): the runner/lead builds phases 1–3 centrally, gates the plan, hands each subagent one company + thesis + registry rules, and each subagent writes only `targets/research-log.parts/<shard>.json` (full log schema, JD snapshots included) — no DB writes, no LinkedIn pass, no user interaction from inside a shard. The lead/runner merges parts with `python scripts/merge_research_parts.py <project>`, dedupes by canonical URL, runs/records the LinkedIn Jobs probe last with `python scripts/probe_linkedin_jobs.py <project>`, then validates and persists once. A failed or partial company shard is retried and reported, never silently dropped.

## Persisting rows

Write every intermediate JSON below **inside `<project>/data/`** (guaranteed writable) — never `/tmp`, `C:\tmp`, or an absolute OS path; on Windows those fail (FileNotFoundError/PermissionError) and a broken persist step silently drops the rows you captured. Persist **all** captured rows (one upsert, or resumable batches kept under `<project>/data/`) — a partial write is how a run ends up with far fewer rows than it found.

1. Ensure store exists: `python scripts/init_db.py`; check project.json for any external sheet copy.
2. Write candidate rows to `<project>/data/rows.json`. Generate stable URL fields with `python scripts/normalize_job_url.py --json <project>/data/rows.json` or, for one posting, `python scripts/normalize_job_url.py --url <url> --company <company> --title <title>` / `python scripts/normalize_job_url.py <url> <company> <title>`.
3. Persist with `python scripts/persist_job_rows.py <project>/data/rows.json --project <project>` — this canonicalizes URLs, normalizes locations, rejects listing/search URLs and unverified JD placeholders, validates against the current job_list, upserts transactionally, verifies read-after-write, regenerates views, and removes intermediate files.
4. Coverage scaffold: `python scripts/generate_coverage_audit.py <project>` — this creates the mechanical counts/gaps baseline from the merged log; add human-readable notes only after it exists.
5. Mechanics check: `python scripts/validate_research_artifacts.py <project>` — fix every FAIL before reporting (this FAILS the run if any declared seed/preference company is absent from the universe or was never attempted).

## Reporting

Summarize: found vs saved vs skipped (with reason distribution), universe coverage (attempted/total companies per bucket), remaining failed_captures with next steps, and the self-audit's follow-up priorities. Distinguish page-captured facts from your judgments. Suggest `target-job-group-strategy` when coverage is adequate — or another research pass targeting the audit's thin spots when it isn't.

**Every user-named (preference/seed) company gets an explicit line in the summary** — `<Company>: N kept / M skipped / failed_capture (why) / excluded (why)`. Never let a named company vanish from the report: a seed that produced zero saved rows is a *reported outcome with its reason* (e.g. "<Company>: JS careers page, no browser runtime available → failed_capture; retry with the browser connector"), not a silent absence. If a preference company shows 0 saved rows, say so at the top so the user can decide to rerun rather than discovering it by counting rows.
